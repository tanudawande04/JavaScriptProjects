Abdul Kalam (born October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, Indiaâ€”died July 27, 2015, Shillong)Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs. He was president of India from 2002 to 2007.A.P.J Abdul Kalam was the 11th President of India. His work with the Defence Research and Development Organisation in developing military equipment like missiles and a civilian space programme earned him the title of Missile Man of India.He thus came to be known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. He also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, the first since the original nuclear test by India in 1974.
